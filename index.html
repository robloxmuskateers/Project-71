campus life

The campus life on Hogwarts is extremely comfortable and promising. It has many hallways and classrooms. All are roomy and have lots of space to move around. There are multiple courtyard where studants can go to enjoy their free time. We have very educated teachers, who are well versed in wand spells. There are four houses at Hogwarts. A house is like your family at the school. You will do classes with them and share a commonroom with them. At the starting of your seven year education, you will be sorted into a house by the sorting hat. You will go home for the summer holidays, and the choice to go home during the winter holidays and easter holidays. You will attend classes every weekday to learn from some our teachers. The commonrooms for each of the houses are comfortable, ranging from the tallest tower to the deepest dungeon. In each commonroom, there will be multiple dormitories for each gender and year of education. 5 boys/girls will be in each dormitory respectivly, and all will be in the same year of education in each dormitory.

school achievements

Proud Keeper Of The Philosopher's Stone
The Philosopher's Stone

The Philosopher's Stone, a substance created to make an elixer of life, making the drinker immortal. Every metal the stone touched turned to gold too. It was originally being held at Gringotts Bank, but the Headmaster of Hogwarts, Professor Dumbledore, thought the Stone was not safe there and had it brought to Hogwarts for safe-keeping. The same day it was extracted, there was a break-in at Gringotts, though nothing was taken. It looked as if the robber was looking for the Stone. Later in the year, the robber turned up, but the Stone was bravely protectod by a certain Mr. Harry Potter. Professor Dumbledore Arrived in time to kill the intruder and save eleven-year-old Harry, and the Stone was destroyed.

Winner Of The Triwizard Tournament
The Triwizard Tournament

Hogwarts school of Witchcraft and Wizardy was the host for the first Triwizard Tournament for a long time. The three schools competing were Beauxbatons, Durmstrang and, of course, Hogwarts. One representative from each scool was supposed to compete, but because of a mix up, two chapions were selected for Hogwarts by the Triwizard Cup. The two champions were Cedric Diggory, in his seventh year, and Harry Potter, in his fourth year. The champion for Beauxbatons was Fleur Delacouer, in her seventh, and for Durmstrang was a popular Victor Krum, also in his seventh. They all competed in three tasks, the first being dragons. They had to get a golden egg from a dragon nest, and all complied. The second task was to find the mer-people village in the black lake and save one of their friends. Mr. Potter ended up saving two of the four, since Fleur Delacour didn't show up. It seemed as she was pulled by a grindlyow, and was out for the task, there was no harm done to her or to any of the hostages. The final task was a hedge maze, and Harry and Cedric got to the cup first. After an unfortunate event, the return of Voldemort, Mr. DIggory suffered an early death. Harry Potter survived and brought his body back. Harry Ended up winning the tournament.

Independance From The Dark Arts
Independence

Three years after the Triwizard Tournament incedint, Harry and his two friends, Hermione Granger and Ronald Weasley, were adventuring with him to destroy all of Voldemort's seven souls. They destoyed the first six in an endless hunt, and returned to Hogwarts, which was under the control of Voldemort himself, to face him. There was an epic battle where students and teachers alike fought to rid their school of the Death Eaters (Voldemort's followers). The final battle occured when Harry and Voldemort had a wand fight, and Mr. Potter fought and won. Voldemort perished, and Hogwarts, and the world, was freed.
